Assistive Web Browser for People with ALS

Idea Summary

ALS is a neuro degenerative disorder which leaves person with no or insignificant voluntary movement. There is no cure for ALS. But, hopefully, a person can still use his eyes voluntarily. Thus, we developed a web browser, which a person can use with his eyes to cater his/her day to day tasks such as reading news, checking mails, etc.

Description

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) is a group of rare neurological diseases that mainly involve the nerve cells (neurons) responsible for controlling voluntary muscle movement. Voluntary muscles produce movements like chewing, walking, and talking. The disease is progressive, meaning the symptoms get worse over time. Currently, there is no cure for ALS and no effective treatment to halt, or reverse, the progression of the disease.

Stephen Hawkings also suffered from ALS. Being, a great scientist, he was helped as given a scan based keyboard which could be operated by moving his cheek, a program devised by Intel (ACAT), to help him communicate with machine.He would take days to type a single speech.
We aim to minimise the effort using an Eye Gaze Tracker, to be used as an input modality to communicate faster with machines.

I am Kamalpreet Singh Saluja, Research Assistant at Intelligent Inclusive Interaction Design Laboratory (I3D Lab), Centre for Product Design and Manufacturing (CPDM), Indian Institute of Science(IISc), Bengaluru. Being in a premier design research lab, where culture of innovation and interdisciplinary outlook is embedded in its genes, it allowed me to learn from pioneers in the field and contribute to multiple domains such as Virtual Reality and Augmented Reality, Assistive Technologies, Multimodal Interaction, Data Visualization, Internet of Things (IoT), Smart manufacturing and Sustainable Design.

In my current role as a research assistant, I review the development of hypotheses and research protocols for data collection. In a nutshell, I have worked on designing and evaluating various Proof of Concepts for Man-Machine Interaction. I coordinated studies in Virtual Reality Systems to explore Multimodal Interaction Paradigms. I do regular field visits to understand and consolidate requirements for people having Severe Speech and Motor Impairment. It rekindles my spirit of research when I actually see the benefits of the system developed by me for their daily activities. I have hands-on experience working with various types of Eye-Trackers. I designed various algorithms using eye-gaze to explore it as interaction modality. I designed several inclusive user interfaces for aviation and automotive domain and evaluated its effectiveness through real-time physiological cognitive load assessment. I coordinated and monitored data collection through various methodologies to conduct sophisticated qualitative and quantitative data analysis.

I have been awarded “Best Presenter Award” at 3rd International Conference on Graphics and Signal Processing (ICGSP’19) for my presentations. For my work on "Comparing Two Webcam based Eye Gaze Trackers for Users with Severe Speech and Motor Impairment" at 7th International Conference on Research Into Design (ICoRD'19), which I was co-authored, received the Distinguished Paper Award. In my previous role as a research intern, I developed a toolkit to generate visual narratives for high dimensional Life Cycle Assessment (LCA). I also assisted in conducting usability studies and data analysis for the same.
